Instruction
1. Creating subscriptions and filters.
A subscription involves tracking or monitoring transactions for an address that you specify.
To create a subscription, click on "Subscribe" at the top. You will be taken to the subscription creation page where you will need to fill in some information:
* Transaction Direction: transactions sent or received by the address.
* The address itself, which will be added to the monitoring service, and information about whose transactions you will receive.
* Fields CONFIRMED, UNCONFIRMED, FAILED: these are the statuses of the transactions that you want to receive. Note: leaving all fields empty is equivalent to selecting all of them.
Click the "Subscribe" button. You will be taken to the additional filters page for your subscription. You don't need to fill in the fields if you want to receive all transactions for the specified direction and address. You can specify filters:
* Sender and receiver address fields (useful if you want to track transactions between two addresses).
* Minimum and maximum amount of currency transferred in ETH. Note: this specifically refers to currency transfer from one address to another. Internal transfers, such as calling a smart contract to exchange tokens, and subsequent recipient actions are not tracked.
* Time interval of the transactions you are interested in. Note: the date and time are specified in UTC+0 without the option for time zones.
Click "Subscribe" to complete the subscription creation.
2. Subscription list
The "Subscriptions" page lists all your registered subscriptions in a table format. The table columns include:
* Type: subscription type. It characterizes the types of subscriptions you will receive: sent (FROM_ADDRESS) or received (TO_ADDRESS).
* Address: the address you have subscribed to. By clicking on it, you will be taken to the page of received transactions. Note: addresses specified when filling out the filter page are not considered.
* Statuses: the statuses of transactions you are tracking.
* With Filter: whether any filters are applied to the subscription.
* Charts: clicking on this cell will take you to the charts page for your specific subscription.
* Delete: clicking on this cell will delete the subscription and all stored information about it. This action is irreversible.
Note: If another user has a similar subscription to yours with similar filters, the information will be retained.
3. Transactions
The transaction table is not self-updating. To receive new information, refresh the page.
The top menu contains filters for all transactions in the table:
* Using "Table fields" you can disable or enable the display of columns in it.
* Using "Filters" you can select transactions by the minimum and maximum amount of ETH, by the date and time of the transaction.
* Using "Order By" you can sort transactions in descending (desc) or ascending (asc) order by a parameter you specify.
4. Charts
On this page, you can find charts of transferred currency in ETH per block in the network, and gas prices also for each block.
The information is automatically compressed, so the main purpose of the charts is to see large spikes or drops in values over time.
